The Anatomy Maps feature is built for medical, nursing, biology, and life science students who need to learn not just what structures exist, but how they're organized and where they fit within larger systems. Paste a passage from a medical textbook or anatomical description, and LaMaina.ai extracts every named structure and body system, organizes them, and generates a labeled anatomical illustration — giving you a visual reference to anchor the terminology you're studying.

## How to Generate an Anatomy Map

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the Study page and scroll to Anatomy Maps">
    Navigate to the `/study` page in your app and scroll down to the **Anatomy Map** section. You can generate a map independently or as part of a broader study session.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter a descriptive title">
    Type a title that identifies the subject of your map — for example, **"Circulatory System"**, **"The Human Brain"**, or **"Plant Cell Structures"**. The title gives the AI context for how to frame the illustration.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste anatomical content">
    Paste your source material — a medical textbook excerpt, lecture notes covering anatomical structures, or any text that names and describes biological components. The more specific the structural names in your text, the richer the output map will be.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Create Map">
    Click the **Create Map** button. The AI identifies body systems and their structures from your text, then generates a labeled anatomical diagram.
  </Step>
  <Step title="View your anatomy map">
    Your map is displayed with labeled structures, organized by body system, alongside an AI-generated anatomical illustration.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What's in an Anatomy Map

Each anatomy map LaMaina.ai generates includes:

- **Body systems list** — the major systems identified in your material (e.g. cardiovascular, nervous, skeletal)
- **Structures within each system** — individual organs, tissues, or components extracted from your text and grouped under their parent system
- **Brief descriptions** — short explanations of each structure's role or characteristics, drawn from your source material
- **AI-generated anatomical illustration** — a labeled diagram that visually represents the structures and their spatial relationships

## 3D Anatomy Viewer

In addition to generated anatomy maps, LaMaina.ai includes a **3D Anatomy Viewer** at `/anatomy3d`. Use it to interactively explore organ structures in three dimensions. The viewer is a standalone tool — you can visit it at any time, independent of a study session, to orient yourself before studying a new system or verify what a structure looks like after reading about it.

<Tip>
  For the most detailed and accurate maps, paste content from medical textbooks or formal anatomical descriptions that use precise terminology. Text that names specific structures, organs, and body systems — rather than describing them only in general terms — gives the AI the most to work with.
</Tip>

<Note>
  Anatomy maps aren't limited to human biology. You can use this feature for **animal biology, botany, microbiology**, or any subject that involves named structures organized into systems. If your material describes the vascular system of a plant or the organ systems of a frog, the AI will extract and map those structures just as effectively.
</Note>
