Quick answers to the most common questions about LaMaina.ai. If you run into a problem rather than a question, see the [Troubleshooting](/troubleshooting) page.

## General

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What is LaMaina.ai?">
    LaMaina.ai is a self-hosted AI study platform that transforms pasted text into interactive study tools. Paste any text-based material and generate:

    - **Summaries** — concise overviews of your content
    - **Quizzes** — up to 50 AI-generated questions with personalised feedback
    - **Presentations** — 5-slide decks with AI-generated images
    - **Flow charts** — visual diagrams of processes and hierarchies
    - **Anatomy maps** — structured visual breakdowns for biological subjects

    Because LaMaina.ai is self-hosted, your data stays in your own infrastructure.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What kind of material works best?">
    LaMaina.ai works with any text-based content. It performs particularly well with:

    - Textbook chapters
    - Lecture notes
    - Articles and blog posts
    - Research papers and abstracts
    - Study guides and outlines

    For best results, paste **500 to 5,000 words** of well-structured text. Material with clear headings, bullet points, or numbered lists gives the AI more structure to work with, especially for flow charts and quizzes.

    <Tip>
      Avoid pasting raw HTML, code listings, or heavily formatted documents. Strip the content down to plain text before pasting for the most reliable results.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is there a word limit?">
    Yes. Each study session supports up to **10,000 words**. If your material is longer, split it into multiple sessions by chapter or topic.

    For example:
    - Session 1 → Introduction & Chapter 1
    - Session 2 → Chapters 2–3
    - Session 3 → Chapters 4–5

    All sessions are saved and accessible from your History page.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Are my study sessions saved?">
    Yes. All sessions are stored in your database and accessible from the **History** page at `/history`. You can return to any previous session to review your quiz answers, re-read summaries, or re-take questions.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Quizzes

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How many questions will be generated?">
    LaMaina.ai generates up to **50 questions per session**. The AI aims for comprehensive coverage of your material, so the actual number of questions will vary depending on the length and density of the content you paste.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I retake a quiz?">
    Yes. Navigate to any previous session from the **History** page and answer the questions again. Each attempt is recorded separately, so you can track your improvement over time.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I answer questions by voice?">
    Yes. Click the microphone icon on the quiz screen to switch to speech-to-text input. Speak your answer clearly and it will be transcribed automatically.

    Voice input uses your browser's built-in speech recognition API. For the best experience, use **Chrome** or **Edge** — they have the most reliable and accurate speech recognition support.

    <Note>
      Voice input requires microphone permissions in your browser. If the microphone icon is greyed out, check that you have granted the site permission to access your microphone.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Does the AI really evaluate my answer?">
    Yes. Each answer you submit is sent to the LLM along with the original study material for context. The AI compares your response to the correct answer and provides **specific, personalised feedback** — not just a right/wrong result. This means you get an explanation of what you got right, what you missed, and how to think about the concept.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Features

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How many slides does a presentation have?">
    Each presentation contains **5 slides**. Every slide includes a title, bullet points summarising the key ideas, and an AI-generated image relevant to the content.

    <Note>
      AI-generated images require a DALL-E 3 compatible LLM provider. If your provider does not support image generation, slides will still be created but without images. See the [Troubleshooting](/troubleshooting) page for details.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do flow charts require a specific format?">
    No specific format is required, but the quality of the output depends on how structured your input is. Material with a natural hierarchy — outlines, numbered processes, or concept maps — produces the clearest and most useful flow charts.

    Dense, unstructured prose will still work, but the AI may not capture every relationship. If the result looks unclear, try summarising the key steps or concepts in a bulleted list first, then paste that as your input.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I use Anatomy Maps for non-human biology?">
    Yes. Anatomy Maps work for any biological subject, not just human anatomy. You can use them for:

    - Animal anatomy (vertebrate and invertebrate)
    - Plant biology and cell structure
    - Microbiology and cellular processes
    - Any subject with a structured, part-to-whole relationship

    Paste descriptive text about the organism or system and the AI will generate a structured visual breakdown.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Self-Hosting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I use a different LLM provider?">
    Yes. LaMaina.ai works with any **OpenAI-compatible API**. To use a different provider, set the following environment variables in your `.env` file:

    ```env
    OPENAI_API_BASE_URL=https://your-provider.example.com/v1
    OPENAI_API_KEY=your-api-key
    LLM_DEFAULT_MODEL=your-preferred-model
    ```

    Popular compatible providers include Groq, Together AI, Mistral, and Anthropic (via compatibility layers). Check your provider's documentation for the correct base URL.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I use a local AI model with Ollama?">
    Yes, Ollama is supported for local inference. To use it, point `OPENAI_API_BASE_URL` at your Ollama server:

    ```env
    OPENAI_API_BASE_URL=http://localhost:11434/v1
    OPENAI_API_KEY=ollama
    LLM_DEFAULT_MODEL=llama3
    ```

    Keep in mind these limitations with local Ollama models:

    - **Quiz generation** requires `response_format: json_schema` (structured JSON output). Most Ollama models do not support this — quiz features may not work reliably.
    - **Image generation** is not supported. Slides and charts will render without AI-generated images.

    Summaries, flow charts, and anatomy maps generally work well with capable local models.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What database does LaMaina.ai use?">
    LaMaina.ai uses **MySQL 8.0+**. Any MySQL-compatible service works, including:

    - Self-hosted MySQL 8.0
    - [TiDB](https://tidbcloud.com/)
    - [PlanetScale](https://planetscale.com/)
    - Amazon RDS for MySQL
    - Azure Database for MySQL

    Set your connection string in `.env`:

    ```env
    DATABASE_URL="mysql://user:password@host:3306/dbname"
    ```
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is my data private when self-hosting?">
    Yes. When you self-host LaMaina.ai, all your study sessions, quiz results, and user accounts are stored in **your own database** on your own infrastructure. No study data is sent to LaMaina.ai servers.

    The only data sent externally is the content you paste into a study session, which is sent to your **configured LLM provider** (for example, OpenAI) for AI processing. If data privacy is a priority, use a local Ollama model so that no content leaves your network.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
