By the end of this guide you will have created a study session, reviewed an AI-generated summary, completed a quiz, and seen your results in the progress dashboard — all in under five minutes. No configuration is required to follow along with the cloud version of LaMaina.ai.

<Tip>
  Start with a focused piece of text between **500 and 2,000 words** for your first session. Shorter material lets you see every feature — summary, questions, and feedback — in one quick pass without being overwhelmed.
</Tip>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open LaMaina.ai">
    Navigate to the LaMaina.ai app in your browser. If you already have an account, log in at `/login`. If you're new, select **Register** on the same page to create a free account — it only takes a moment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a Study Session">
    Go to `/study` and click **New Session**. Give your session a descriptive title (for example, `Chapter 5 Biology`) so you can find it easily later. Paste your study material into the text area — you can paste up to **10,000 words** in a single session.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the AI Summary">
    As soon as you save the session, LaMaina.ai generates a structured summary of your material. Read through it to confirm the key concepts have been captured correctly before moving on to the quiz.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate Questions">
    Click **Generate Questions** to create a comprehensive quiz drawn directly from your material. The AI analyses the content and produces questions that cover the most important concepts, facts, and relationships in the text.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Answer the Quiz">
    Work through the questions at your own pace. After you submit each answer, the AI evaluates your response and provides immediate feedback — including an explanation of the correct answer and why it matters.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Track Your Progress">
    Visit `/history` to see every session you've created. From there you can review your summary, check your score, and resume any session you haven't finished. Use this dashboard to identify topics where you need more practice.
  </Step>
</Steps>
